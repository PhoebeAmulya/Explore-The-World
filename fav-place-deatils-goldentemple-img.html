Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is a sacred Sikh gurdwara located in Amritsar, Punjab, India. It is the most important spiritual site in Sikhism and attracts millions of pilgrims and tourists annually. The temple was founded by Guru Ram Das, the fourth Sikh Guru, in 1581, and its construction was completed by his successor, Guru Arjan. The temple is renowned for its stunning architecture, with its golden dome and walls, which are made of marble and covered in gold leaf. The temple is surrounded by a large, serene pool of water known as the Amrit Sarovar, which is believed to have healing properties. The Golden Temple is open to people of all faiths, symbolizing the Sikh principles of equality, humility, and service. Inside the temple, the Guru Granth Sahib, the central religious scripture of Sikhism, is recited continuously. The temple complex also houses the Akal Takht, the highest seat of earthly authority of the Khalsa, the collective body of all Sikhs. The Golden Temple is not only a place of worship but also a symbol of peace and brotherhood.
